Seiun Sky (1995-2011) was a prominent Japanese Thoroughbred racehorse best known for winning two legs of the Japanese Triple Crown in 1998. Over a career of 13 starts, he achieved 7 wins and earned over ¥610 million.

Racing Career & "Golden Generation"

Seiun Sky was a key member of the 1998 "Golden Generation" alongside legendary rivals like Special Week and Grass Wonder.

  • Triple Crown Success (1998): He won the Satsuki Sho (2,000 Guineas) by a nose and later dominated the Kikuka Sho (St. Leger), where he set a then-world record for 3,000 meters (3:03.2). He missed the Triple Crown after finishing 4th in the Tokyo Yushun (Japanese Derby).
  • Tactics: Known as the "Trickster," he was a master front-runner. His jockey, Norihiro Yokoyama, used "pace manipulation"—running fast at the start, slowing down significantly in the middle to lulls rivals into a false sense of security, and then sprinting away at the end.
  • Challenges: Despite his success, he had a notorious dislike for starting gates, often refusing to enter and causing significant delays.

Stallion Career & Legacy

  • Retirement: After suffering from chronic tendonitis in late 1999, he was sidelined for over a year. He made a brief, unsuccessful comeback in 2001 before retiring.
  • Breeding: He was not initially considered a top-tier stallion due to his "low-value" pedigree. However, his owner paired him with the champion mare Nishino Flower to preserve his bloodline. This eventually produced the 2022 Nakayama Daishogai (Steeplechase G1) winner, Nishino Daisy.
  • Death: Seiun Sky passed away on August 16, 2011, at age 16. While the official cause was a heart attack, it was reported he fell and hit his head in his stable.
